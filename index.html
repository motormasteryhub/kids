Behind the Hub

Built by a Team of Three. Backed by the Best Research in Child Development.

This guide didn't come from guesswork or Pinterest boards. Our three-person team spent months deep in the landmark works of four of the most respected minds in early childhood psychology and education — researchers whose discoveries fundamentally changed how we understand how children focus, move, and learn.

After studying thousands of pages of their research and clinical observations, one thing became undeniable: the skills children need most — focus, fine motor control, coordination, emotional regulation — are built through short, consistent, hands-on play. Not screens. Not passive learning. Real movement. That insight became this guide.

The 4 Research Pillars Our Team Studied

🧠
Dr. Jean Piaget Cognitive Development Theory — stages of how children build understanding through physical interaction with the world
🌱
Dr. Maria Montessori Montessori Method — the role of purposeful, hands-on activity in developing independence, focus and fine motor mastery
🤝
Dr. Lev Vygotsky Zone of Proximal Development — how guided, playful challenges stretch a child's abilities just beyond their current level
💛
Dr. Stanley Greenspan DIR/Floortime Model — how emotional connection during play is the foundation of attention, regulation and motor development
